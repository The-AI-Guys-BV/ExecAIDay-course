# execaiday.com — competitor + market research

Research compiled 2026-05-14 by a critique agent benchmarking ExecAIDay (white-glove personal AI install service) against the install-services market. To be moved to the execaiday.com website repository once it's set up.

## Direct competitor benchmarks (concrete numbers)

### Concierge Studio — conciergestudio.ai
The closest direct competitor. Same audience (founders / CEOs / operators / investors), same proposition (personal AI architect).

- **Essentials — $5,000.** Single 4–6 hour build session.
- **Professional — $7,500.** 3-month retainer.
- **Sovereign — $15,000.** 6-month retainer with in-person setup.
- Quantified claim: *"12+ hours per week saved on email triage and research alone."*
- One named architect testimonial.

### Athena — athena.com (premium executive assistant subscription)
- **$3,000/month** printed openly on marketing page.
- Onboarding: intake survey + 60-min onboarding call + dedicated Partnership Manager + replacement guarantee.
- Named client roster (Stripe, Goldman, Sequoia).
- Specific revenue claim from named client (Jacob Tingen, +$200k).
- Aggregate stat: *"40–50% more time on revenue-generating work."*

### Notion certified consultants
- Published consensus band: **$5k–$15k per workspace build** for executive setups.
- Project-based pricing is the norm; hourly ($100–$200) is the floor, not the headline.

### Fractional Chief of Staff market
- **$8k–$20k/month** for 10–25 hrs/week.
- Pitches "ex-consultants / former operators" as social proof.
- Audience overlaps almost completely with ExecAIDay.

### David Allen Co / GTD
- Certified-coach network model.
- Productized executive 1:1 plus train-the-trainer for orgs.
- The "methodology from the source" play is the credibility move.

### Boutique "done for you AI" agencies
- Published consensus bands:
  - **<$10k** = strategy only
  - **$10k–$25k** = pilot
  - **$25k–$60k** = "done means done"
- This is the price grammar ExecAIDay's offering fits into.

### White-glove smart home / AV installers (pattern source, not direct competition)
- Useful for service-language proof: booties, dust cloths, "complete in one day," named-certified technicians, lifecycle support.
- Category lives or dies on physical-presence trust signals.

## What this market does that ExecAIDay's positioning doesn't yet

1. **Publishes a price band.** Everyone serious does (Concierge Studio $5k/$7.5k/$15k; Notion $5k–$15k; Athena $3k/mo). Absence reads as *"if you have to ask."*
2. **Quantifies time-to-value.** Concierge: 12+ hrs/week saved. Athena: 40–50% more revenue time. Use real numbers from real engagements.
3. **Shows a named testimonial.** Even one. Concierge Studio has one named-architect quote. Athena names clients with revenue claims. ExecAIDay needs at least one.
4. **Names the engagement contract.** Replacement guarantee, SOC 2, response-time SLA. Aftercare page is good content but contains no warranty language.
5. **Tiers the offer.** Standard / Premium / Sovereign-equivalent with content per tier.
6. **Foregrounds "1:1 in-person."** Premium signal once video has commoditized everything else. Should be in the hero, not in a meta field.
7. **Shows the installer team.** Photo of Tijn, credentials, prior engagements (even anonymized), ideally a logo wall.
8. **States pre-requisite cost transparently.** Claude Max = $200/mo ongoing. Tell buyers up front.
9. **Scope guardrails ("what's not included").** Buyers worry about IT blocking connectors, etc. Address explicitly.
10. **CTA labeled as a buyer deliverable, not buyer homework.** *"Get your AI readiness brief"* not *"Start your intake."*

## What ExecAIDay does well (preserve in marketing positioning)

1. **Deliverable is named and specific** — "The Executive Cockpit." Concierge Studio's deliverable is a fuzzy "custom AI system."
2. **Phase structure is unusually concrete** — phase outcomes are operational language, not marketing.
3. **The intake-before-install loop is genuinely sophisticated** — 30-min pre-workshop profile pre-tunes the vault. Concierge has 45-min discovery call only.
4. **Aftercare reads like a real contract.** Quarterly check-in standing prompt, named cadence, anti-pattern guidance.
5. **"Not a course" framing is sharp.** Defensible position.
6. **Vendor lock minimal.** Vault outlasts Cowork-the-product. Buyer-friendly stance most boutiques don't make.
7. **Methodology depth is real.** Layered `claude.md`, GTD skill pack, MCP, scheduled tasks. Most competitors at this price point ship Zapier flows.

## Priority fixes for the marketing site (effort estimates)

1. **Price band on the homepage.** ~2 hrs. Three tiers OR "from €X."
2. **One named testimonial with quantified outcome.** ~1 day (outreach to past attendees). Anonymous-but-credible (*"Managing Partner, mid-cap PE fund (NDA)"*) is fallback.
3. **Quantify the deliverable.** ~3 hrs. *"By end of day two: cockpit refreshing in <5s, ≥4 connectors live, ≥3 scheduled tasks running, ≥1 custom skill built."*
4. **Total-cost-of-ownership block.** ~2 hrs. Workshop fee + required Claude Max subscription + optional add-ons + aftercare cost. CFO-adjacent transparency.
5. **Convert deliverable section to verifiable checklist.** ~4 hrs. 23 items, scannable.
6. **Show install team.** ~1 day. Photo, link to PhD work, prior corporate engagements, anonymized client logo if needed.
7. **Reframe intake CTA as buyer deliverable.** ~2 hrs. *"Get your readiness brief"* not *"Start your intake."*
8. **FAQ — 3 load-bearing objections.** ~4 hrs. IT blocking connectors / Mac→PC switch / Anthropic changes Cowork.

Items 1–4 together: under 3 days. That's the version that closes materially more deals than the current curriculum-site cover does.
