Cowork Essentials

The fast-paced track. Dr. van der Zant walks you through M01 (full install) live, then guides you through the rest. By the end you have a personalized AI workspace, connectors to your real data, a chief-of-staff brief, 7 custom skills, and the Daily Command Center. 12 essential moves, each one direct: what to do, what prompt to try, what comes next. Whenever you want a deeper dive on any module, type /teach <module> in Cowork and the tutor walks you through it patiently. Or jump straight to the full course.

1. Welcome + Foundations (M01)

Open Claude Desktop, switch to the Cowork tab. Find your workshop Cowork Project. Your vault and work folder are mounted. Type /teach. You should see a list of 16 modules — your safety net.

Verify: Type /teach M01 — expect a patient walkthrough of the M01 concepts.

Deep dive: type /teach M01 in Cowork, or open the full M01 page.

2. Sharpen any prompt with R-C-T-F-C (M02)

The best prompts have five elements: Role, Context, Task, Format, Constraints. Skip any one and the output is generic. If you don't want to remember all five:

/help-me-prompt help me write a follow-up email to a candidate I want to hire

Claude rewrites it in R-C-T-F-C structure. Ship the rewrite.

Deep dive: type /teach M02 in Cowork, or open the full M02 page.

3. Build a live artifact (M04)

An artifact is a generated output that renders inline. Live artifacts pull fresh data each time you open them.

Build me an interactive page showing the days of the week in a 2x4 grid,
each in a different color, with today highlighted.

Renders right there. Close it, reopen — it refreshes. The Daily Command Center (step 12) is the same idea, scaled up.

Verify: Close and reopen the artifact — expect it to show fresh data.

Deep dive: type /teach M04 in Cowork, or open the full M04 page.

4. Operate websites with Claude in Chrome (M05)

Open Chrome (or Brave). Click the Claude extension icon — sidebar opens on the right. Open any portal you actually use (CRM, dashboard, vendor portal). In the sidebar:

Pull the next renewal date from the customer profile open in this tab.

Or record a 3-step workflow once and replay it on demand. Or open 6 competitor sites in tabs and ask Claude to compile a comparison.

Verify: Ask Claude to extract a specific field from the current page — expect accurate data pulled from the live page.

Deep dive: type /teach M05 in Cowork, or open the full M05 page.

5. Write your chief-of-staff brief (M06)

The highest-leverage file in the workshop. Once it exists, every conversation inherits your voice, priorities, stakeholders, and tone. In Cowork:

Walk me through writing my chief-of-staff brief. Save the result to
my vault as About Me.md, and add a pointer in claude.md.

Claude interviews you for a few minutes — role, voice, stakeholders, what you avoid. Output is a 30-line brief that personalizes everything Claude does for you afterward.

Verify: Open About Me.md in your vault — expect a complete CoS brief in your voice.

Deep dive: type /teach M06 in Cowork, or open the full M06 page.

6. Walk out with 7 custom skills (M07)

You've already used /teach and /help-me-prompt — those are skills (slash commands). The workshop installs 7 core skills. To build your own:

/skill-creator

Claude interviews you about a task you do often, drafts the skill, writes it to your machine. Then it's a slash command you can invoke forever. The autonomy moment.

Verify: Type / in a Cowork chat — expect the skill to run.

Deep dive: type /teach M07 in Cowork, or open the full M07 page.

7. Use Claude to read and write your vault (M08)

Your vault is mounted in your Cowork Project. That means Claude reads every note and writes back. Use Claude to capture, synthesize, and organize. Try:

Write a 5-line journal entry about how the workshop is going so far,
in journal voice. Save to today's daily note in my vault.

Open Obsidian — the note is there. Same workflow scales: ask Claude to read a PDF and save key points under References/, or summarize your meeting notes from a transcript.

Verify: Open your vault in Obsidian — expect the new note, visible and rendered.

Deep dive: type /teach M08 in Cowork, or open the full M08 page.

8. Folders, projects, and the two files Claude reads automatically (M09)

Two files shape how Claude behaves in any folder:

  • claude.md — per-folder context. Claude reads this first. The closer to the file Claude is editing, the higher priority it wins.
  • memory.md — patterns Cowork learns over time. Claude appends; you can edit by hand.

To scaffold a new project — bounded work with Source Materials / Working Files / Deliverables, plus a tailored claude.md:

/new-project board-deck-q3

Claude interviews you (outcome, stakeholders, deadline, tone), creates the project folder, writes a claude.md that inherits your CoS brief plus project specifics. Every conversation in that folder gets project context for free.

Verify: Check your work folder — expect the new project subfolder with source/working/deliverables structure.

Deep dive: type /teach M09 in Cowork, or open the full M09 page.

9. Verify your connectors (M10)

Dr. van der Zant already authenticated Gmail, M365, Calendar, Drive, Slack, and custom apps. Confirm in Customize → Connectors — all show Connected. Try one:

/email-triage today

Claude classifies your inbox into urgent / reply / read / archive / delete. Or:

/drafts-inbox new

Claude scans recent emails needing replies, drafts them all to a review surface.

Verify: Type /email-triage today — expect a structured breakdown of your inbox.

Deep dive: type /teach M10 in Cowork, or open the full M10 page.

Bonus: Prep for any meeting in 30 seconds

Before you walk into a meeting:

/meeting-prep

Claude pulls the calendar event, reads your calendar context, scans recent emails and Slack for relevant chatter, reads your CoS brief, and surfaces: agenda highlights, who's attending, what you own, what's changed since last time. Thirty seconds to go from cold to prepared.

10. Make a branded PowerPoint (M11)

Dr. van der Zant installed the Word, Excel, PowerPoint Claude add-ins. Open any recent on-brand PowerPoint deck of yours plus a blank one. In the blank deck's Claude sidebar:

Open .pptx. Extract the visual style — fonts,
color palette, logo placement, slide layouts. Then build me a 5-slide deck
on [your topic] using exactly that style. Don't copy content — only the look.

Output looks like YOUR company's deck. Cross-app context: do an Excel analysis first, then have PowerPoint Claude pull it in.

Verify: Check the output deck — expect branded styling matching your reference deck.

Deep dive: type /teach M11 in Cowork, or open the full M11 page.

11. Schedule Claude to work while you sleep (M12)

In Cowork: Settings → Scheduled tasks → New. Pick a cadence (e.g., Mondays at 8:30am), give it a prompt, send the output to a Slack DM, an email draft, or a vault file. Example:

Tell me what's important this week. Pull from my calendar (next 7 days),
my recent emails (last 48h), and my Slack mentions. Format as a 5-line
brief. Send as a Slack DM to me.

Verify: Check your Slack DM — expect the brief to arrive at the scheduled time.

Deep dive: type /teach M12 in Cowork, or open the full M12 page.

12. Build your Daily Command Center (M14)

The flagship artifact. One live page that pulls calendar, urgent email, drafts waiting, Slack mentions, and yesterday's reflection — all in one view that refreshes every time you open it. In Cowork:

Build me a live artifact called "Daily Command Center" with these tiles:
1. Today's calendar (Calendar connector).
2. Top 3 urgent emails (Gmail / M365).
3. Drafts waiting for me to send.
4. Slack mentions overnight.
5. Yesterday's daily note closing paragraph.

Layout 2 columns. Refresh on every open.

Open this every morning. Done.

Verify: Reopen the artifact multiple times — expect fresh data each time.

Deep dive: type /teach M14 in Cowork, or open the full M14 page.
