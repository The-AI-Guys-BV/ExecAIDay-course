Cowork Essentials

The fast-paced track. Your install is pre-done before you sit down; we verify it live, then move. By the end you have two Cowork Projects (Vault and Workshop), a chief-of-staff brief in your voice, companion knowledge files Claude pulls from, 7 custom skills, scheduled routines running on your behalf, and the Daily Command Center. 14 essential moves, in the order the full course teaches them — what to do, what prompt to try, what comes next. Whenever you want a deeper dive on any module, type /teach <module> in Cowork and the tutor walks you through it patiently. Or jump straight to the full course.

1. Welcome + install verification (M01)

Your install was pre-done before you arrived. We verify together, fast. Anything red — flag your assistant, they fix it now and you keep moving.

Vocabulary you'll meet in this hour

The five words below show up over and over. Read once now; we'll use them throughout.

  • Vault. A folder of plain markdown notes. Obsidian is the viewer; Cowork is the active layer that reads and writes the same files. Sits inside a cloud-sync app (Dropbox / OneDrive / Google Drive / iCloud Drive) so it's backed up and reachable from your phone.
  • Cowork Project. An AI workspace. Mounts folders, holds its own conversation history and memory. You can have several. Today you'll create two: Vault (in section 3) for vault-only knowledge work, and Workshop (in section 12) for project deliverables.
  • Mount. Telling a Cowork Project to include a folder in scope. Doesn't copy or move the folder — Claude just gets read/write access. A folder can be mounted in multiple Cowork Projects.
  • Connector. An authenticated bridge from Cowork to one of your services (Gmail, Calendar, Slack, etc.). Pre-authenticated for you before the workshop.
  • Plugin / skill / slash command. A skill is a workflow Cowork can run when you type /<name> — e.g., /teach, /capture. Plugins bundle skills together. The workshop's plugin (execaiday) is pre-installed and gives you /teach plus 25 other skills.

Six checks, in order

  1. Cowork is open. Claude Desktop is running. The Cowork tab is visible in the left sidebar with sections for Customize, Personal plugins, and Cowork Projects.
  2. Obsidian is opened on your vault. Open Obsidian. The file tree on the left shows about-me/, Daily Notes/, References/, Inbox/, plus a claude.md at the vault root.
  3. The Claude browser extension is installed. Open Chrome / Brave / Edge. The Claude icon is pinned in your toolbar (right of the address bar).
  4. The ExecAIDay plugin is installed. Cowork → Customize → Personal plugins → execaiday is in the list and marked Installed.
  5. Connectors are connected. Cowork → Customize → Connectors → every service you use shows a green Connected badge.
  6. Office add-ins are installed. Open Word, Excel, PowerPoint. Each app's ribbon shows a Claude icon. Clicking it opens the Claude sidebar inside the app.

Then in any Cowork chat:

/teach

Expected: Claude lists 17 modules. If you get "Unknown command" or only a partial list, the plugin install didn't complete — flag your assistant.

Verify: /teach works and lists all 17 modules. Six checks all green.

Deep dive: type /teach M01 in Cowork, or open the full M01 page.

3. Create your first Cowork Project — "Vault" (M03)

This is where Claude meets your vault. In Cowork, top-left of the sidebar:

  1. Click + New Project.
  2. Name it: Vault.
  3. Click Create. The new project opens.
  4. In Mounted folders, click + Add folder. Browse to your vault folder. Confirm.

Only mount the vault — not your work folder. The work folder gets mounted in a different Cowork Project in section 12.

In a chat in this new project, paste:

What folders are mounted in this Cowork Project, and what's at the
root of each?

Claude lists your vault and the top-level folders inside.

Then a writing test:

Write a 5-line journal entry about what brought me to this workshop
and what I'm hoping to walk out with. Use journal voice — first person,
direct, brief. Save to today's daily note in my vault
(Daily Notes/<today>.md).

Watch Obsidian. The note appears or updates in real time. From now on, anything you can describe in a prompt, Claude can do to your vault.

Verify: Open today's daily note in Obsidian — expect Claude's text there. If Claude says it can't find the vault, the mount didn't take — flag your assistant.

4. Write your chief-of-staff brief (M04)

The highest-leverage file in the workshop. Once it exists, every conversation in your Vault Cowork Project inherits your voice, priorities, stakeholders, and tone. In your Vault project:

Walk me through writing my chief-of-staff brief. Save the result to
my vault as About Me.md, and add a one-line pointer in claude.md.

Claude interviews you for a few minutes — role, voice, stakeholders, what you avoid, things you don't want it doing on your behalf. Output is a ~30-line brief that anchors every conversation afterward.

Recognition shock prompt:

Read my About Me brief. Surface 3 things you noticed about me that
aren't obvious from what I just told you.

If at least one observation makes you go "huh, yeah" — the brief is doing its job.

Verify: Open About Me.md in your vault — expect a complete brief in your voice. Open claude.md at the vault root — expect a single line pointing at About Me.md.

Deep dive: type /teach M04 in Cowork, or open the full M04 page.

5. Companion knowledge files (M05)

The brief is short on purpose. The companions go deep. In your Vault Cowork Project:

Expand about-me/long-form.md with deeper context — role history,
stakeholders by name where appropriate, work patterns, things I care
about deeply, things I refuse to do. Aim for 1-2 pages.

Then writing rules:

Pull writing rules from our chief-of-staff interview into
about-me/writing-rules.md. Two sections: Always and Never.
Each rule one line, concrete and testable.

Then the References/ structure:

Set up a References/ folder structure with subfolders for brand,
people, tools, and products. Add a small claude.md in each explaining
what goes there.

Inheritance test in a fresh chat:

Draft a 3-paragraph email to my board chair updating them on Q3.
Use what you know about me. List the files you read.

The draft sounds like you. Claude lists About Me.md, about-me/long-form.md, about-me/writing-rules.md.

Verify: Open about-me/ in Obsidian — expect long-form.md and writing-rules.md populated. Open References/ — expect the subfolder structure.

6. Sharpen any prompt with R-C-T-F-C (M06)

11. Make a branded PowerPoint (M11)

The Word, Excel, PowerPoint Claude add-ins are installed. The branded-deck pattern is two stages: extract the branding once and save it, then reuse it for every new deck.

Stage 1 — Extract branding from a reference deck (one-time)

  1. Use your Vault Cowork Project so the saved branding lands in the vault and any future Cowork Project that mounts the vault can reach it.
  2. Add your reference PowerPoint to the vault. Pick a recent on-brand deck (board update, investor pitch — any topic, only the styling matters). Drop it at <vault>/References/brand/source-deck.pptx.
  3. Ask Claude to extract and save the branding — paste this in your Vault chat: 
    Read <vault>/References/brand/source-deck.pptx. Extract the visual style:
- Brand colors (with hex codes)
- Fonts for headings and body
- Logo placement (which corner, what size)
- Slide layouts (title slide, content slide, section divider)
- Header / footer treatment

Save the spec as <vault>/References/brand/branding.md.
Save the logo image as <vault>/References/brand/logo.png.
Confirm both files when written.

Verify: open <vault>/References/brand/branding.md in Obsidian — expect a clean spec listing colors, fonts, layouts; logo.png sits next to it.

You now have a reusable brand spec. Do this once.

Stage 2 — Build a new deck using saved branding

  1. Switch to your Workshop Cowork Project (created in section 12 — if you haven't done section 12 yet, jump there first, then come back).
  2. Confirm the vault is mounted in Workshop. Both your work folder and your vault should appear in Mounted folders. The branding spec lives in the vault.
  3. Open a blank PowerPoint in your work folder.
  4. In the PowerPoint Claude sidebar, ask: 
    Build me a 5-slide deck on [your real topic — e.g., "Q3 board update on
revenue trajectory"]. Use the branding spec at
<vault>/References/brand/branding.md and the logo at
<vault>/References/brand/logo.png. One bold takeaway per slide,
one supporting data point, no clutter.

Verify: the deck Claude generates uses your brand colors, fonts, and logo placement — recognizably yours, not Claude's defaults.

Bonus — Cross-app chain in the same Cowork Project

With Excel + PowerPoint + Word add-ins all attached to the same Workshop Cowork Project, the conversation persists across the three apps:

  1. Open your Q3 financial model in Excel inside the project. Ask Excel-Claude to analyze and surface top trends and risks (writes comments into the relevant cells).
  2. Switch to the PowerPoint deck in the same project. Ask PowerPoint-Claude to "build a slide section based on the Excel analysis we just did." It remembers — no re-explaining.
  3. Switch to a Word memo. Ask it to "draft a 1-page memo summarizing the Excel + PPT work for the board chair." It pulls the same context.

13. Schedule Claude to work while you sleep (M13)

In Cowork: Settings → Scheduled tasks → New. Pick a cadence (e.g., Mondays at 8:30am), give it a prompt, send the output to a Slack DM, an email draft, or a vault file. Example:

Tell me what's important this week. Pull from my calendar (next 7 days),
my recent emails (last 48h), and my Slack mentions. Format as a 5-line
brief. Send as a Slack DM to me.

Verify: Check the scheduled-tasks list — expect your task with the next run time. Wait for the run; check your Slack DM — expect the brief at the scheduled time.

14. Build your Daily Command Center (M15)

The flagship artifact. One live page that pulls calendar, urgent email, drafts waiting, Slack mentions, and yesterday's reflection — all in one view that refreshes every time you open it. In your Vault Cowork Project:

Build me a live artifact called "Daily Command Center" with these tiles:
1. Today's calendar (Calendar connector).
2. Top 3 urgent emails (Gmail / M365).
3. Drafts waiting for me to send.
4. Slack mentions overnight.
5. Yesterday's daily note closing paragraph.

Layout 2 columns. Refresh on every open.

Open this every morning. Done.

Verify: Reopen the artifact multiple times — expect fresh data each time.
