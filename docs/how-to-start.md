# How to Start

If you're reading this on Day 1 of the workshop, your instructor will guide you through everything. This document is for the day after the workshop ends — the morning where you sit at your laptop and have to actually use this for the first time without a room full of help.

## The first thing to do

Open Cowork. Switch to the workshop Cowork Project (the one mounting your vault and your work folder). Type:

```
/teach M01
```

Claude reads the M01 module file from this plugin and walks you through it patiently, one section at a time. It won't skip ahead. It won't summarize. It will wait for you to actually do each step.

If `/teach` doesn't work, the plugin is incompletely installed. Reinstall it from the marketplace and try again.

## The second thing

You will get a morning brief. Either today or tomorrow, depending on when the workshop wrapped. The brief lands via:

1. A Slack post on your phone (mobile notification).
2. A Markdown file in your Cowork folder.
3. A live artifact dashboard inside Cowork.

Open the live artifact. That's your every-morning view from now on. Each tile is one source — calendar, GTD next-actions, Slack mentions, email urgent + drafts, stale waiting-fors. The artifact refreshes every time you open it.

## The third thing

Capture something. Anything. Type:

```
/capture remind me to look at the Q3 numbers before the board meeting
```

It lands in your GTD inbox at `<vault>/gtd/inbox.md`. That's the proof that the system is working.

## What to do this week

- **Each morning:** open the live artifact dashboard. Two minutes. Decide what matters today.
- **As things come up:** `/capture` them. Don't try to organize on the way in.
- **Once a day, ideally end-of-day:** `/process-inbox`. Five minutes. Clarify each item — is it actionable? What's the next action? Who owns it? Skill walks you through one at a time.
- **Once a week, ideally Monday morning:** `/weekly-review`. Reviews stale items, updates project statuses, flags what to push.

## What NOT to do this week

- Don't try to use every skill. Pick three: `/capture`, `/process-inbox`, and one more that solves a real pain you have. Master those before adding more.
- Don't manually edit your GTD vault files when you can avoid it. Always go through Cowork. The skills enforce the GTD methodology; manual edits break the pattern.
- Don't expect everything to feel natural in week one. The skills are deposits in a habit account. Two weeks in, the muscle is there.

## When you get stuck

`/teach <module-name>` re-walks any module you want. Permanent office hours.

For technical issues, your workshop assistant has access for 60 days post-workshop. Slack them.

For things outside the workshop scope (your IT department blocks something, a connector breaks, you want to extend the system), `/skill-creator` builds new skills from scratch. You learned how in M11.
